1. Know the Beach Conditions

  • Check Ocean Alerts: Before heading to the beach, consult the Beach Buddy app for real-time alerts on high waves, storm surges, and tsunami warnings.
  • Observe Weather Conditions: Stay informed about local weather forecasts, wind speeds, and potential storm warnings.

2. Swim Safely

  • Designated Swimming Areas: Always swim in areas that are specifically designated for swimming and monitored by lifeguards.
  • Beware of Rip Currents: Familiarize yourself with rip current safety measures. If caught in one, swim parallel to the shore to escape its grip.
  • Never Swim Alone: Always swim with a buddy or in a group, especially in unfamiliar waters.

3. Follow Beach Regulations

  • Respect Local Rules: Adhere to all posted beach signs and regulations. These are put in place for your safety.
  • Stay Within Safety Zones: Respect the boundaries of swimming and recreational areas marked by buoys or signs.

4. Environmental Awareness

  • Avoid Polluting the Beach: Dispose of waste properly and participate in beach clean-up initiatives to protect marine life and ecosystems.
  • Observe Wildlife: Maintain a safe distance from marine animals and their habitats. Avoid disturbing wildlife, especially nesting sites.

5. Health Precautions

  • Sun Protection: Use sunscreen with a high SPF, wear sunglasses, and a hat to protect against harmful UV rays.
  • Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated, especially in hot weather.
  • Know Local Health Services: Familiarize yourself with the nearest medical facilities or first-aid stations in case of emergencies.

6. Safety Equipment

  • Use Life Jackets: When participating in water sports or activities, always wear a properly fitted life jacket.
  • Keep Safety Gear Handy: Bring along necessary safety equipment, such as whistles, flares, and a first-aid kit.

7. Emergency Procedures

  • Know Emergency Contacts: Save important emergency numbers, including local authorities, beach patrol, and your accommodation.
  • Plan for Emergencies: Familiarize yourself with the nearest exits, emergency services, and evacuation routes.
